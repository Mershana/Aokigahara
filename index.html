Aokigahara is considered a young forest because it was formed about 1,200 years ago.The last major eruption of the Fuji volcano occurred in 1707 and for some reason did not cover one of the slopes of about 3,000 hectares with lava. This area was later overgrown with a dense forest of boxwood, pines, and other conifers. The trees stand practically as a solid wall. The fauna of Aokigahara includes foxes, snakes and dogs.
